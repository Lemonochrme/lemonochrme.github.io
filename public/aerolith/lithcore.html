LithCore
Flight Controller

A custom STM32F405-based flight controller engineered from scratch. No black-box middleware. No third-party flight stack. Complete firmware ownership and register-level hardware control.

In Development STM32F405 168 MHz Cortex-M4F

Design Philosophy

No Off-the-Shelf
Flight Stacks.

Most drone developers build on top of Betaflight, ArduPilot, or PX4. LithCore takes the opposite approach: we own every line of flight-critical code, every register configuration, every interrupt handler.

This means more development time upfront — and dramatically better visibility into system behavior in production. When something fails, we know exactly why.

LithCore is designed to be the brain of the Aerolith ecosystem — interfacing directly with LithDrive ESCs, IMU sensors, and the CineLith airframe electronics through clean, documented protocols.

Firmware

Built on Bare Metal.
Designed for Determinism.

The LithCore firmware is a custom real-time control system. Sensor fusion, attitude estimation, PID control, and motor output are all handled in tightly scheduled interrupt-driven loops.

Sensor Fusion
IMU Processing Pipeline

Raw accelerometer and gyroscope data is processed through a complementary or Mahony filter at a fixed update rate, providing stable attitude estimates even under vibration.

Mahony / Madgwick Quaternion math Anti-aliasing
Control Loop
Cascaded PID Architecture

An outer attitude loop and inner rate loop run at separate update frequencies, providing both stability and responsiveness. Gains are tunable over the debug UART interface.

Cascaded PID Rate + Attitude loops Configurable gains
Communication
DSHOT Motor Protocol

Motor commands are transmitted to LithDrive ESCs over DSHOT, a digital protocol that eliminates calibration and provides precise, latency-free motor control with bidirectional telemetry.

DSHOT300/600 Bidirectional telemetry Zero latency jitter
Safety
Watchdog & Failsafe

Hardware watchdog timers and software-defined failsafes ensure predictable behavior on signal loss, sensor faults, or firmware hangs — critical for operating over people or property.

IWDG / WWDG RC signal timeout Motor cutoff policy
