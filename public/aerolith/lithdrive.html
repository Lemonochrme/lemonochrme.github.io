LithDrive
BLDC Speed Controller

A custom electronic speed controller built on STM32F051, implementing 6-step trapezoidal BLDC commutation with a MOSFET power stage — engineered for precision, efficiency, and native LithCore integration.

In Development STM32F051 6-step Trap. N-ch MOSFET

ESC Design

Power Electronics,
Done Right.

LithDrive implements 6-step trapezoidal BLDC commutation — the most efficient and widely validated control scheme for drone motors. Our gate driver and MOSFET selection are optimized for the switching frequencies and current levels of mid-size drone applications.

Back-EMF zero-crossing detection enables sensorless motor startup and speed feedback without hall sensors, keeping the airframe clean and reducing failure points.

LithDrive is designed to operate natively with LithCore via DSHOT — a digital protocol that eliminates PWM calibration, reduces timing jitter, and enables bidirectional telemetry for real-time current and RPM monitoring.

How It Works

6-Step Trapezoidal
Commutation Explained

Step 1 — Signal
Receiving the Motor Command

LithDrive receives a digital DSHOT throttle command from LithCore. The STM32F051 decodes the frame in real-time using DMA-assisted UART capture, extracting the 11-bit throttle value and telemetry request bit.

DSHOT 11-bit throttle DMA capture
Step 2 — Commutation
6-Step Trapezoidal Sequencing

The motor's three phases are energized in six discrete steps per electrical revolution. Two phases are active at any time; the third is used for back-EMF sensing. The STM32's advanced timers generate complementary PWM signals for the high- and low-side MOSFETs.

Complementary PWM Dead-time insertion Phase sequencing
Step 3 — Sensing
Back-EMF Zero-Crossing Detection

During the floating phase, the STM32 ADC monitors the motor's back-EMF voltage. When it crosses the motor's virtual neutral point, the next commutation step is triggered — enabling sensorless, self-timed operation from idle to full speed.

ADC sampling Virtual neutral ZCD comparator
Step 4 — Telemetry
Closed-Loop Feedback to LithCore

Real-time motor speed (eRPM), phase current (measured via shunt), supply voltage, and temperature are packaged and returned to LithCore on each telemetry cycle for closed-loop control and safety monitoring.

eRPM Current shunt Voltage monitoring NTC temperature
